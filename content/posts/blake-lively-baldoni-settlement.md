# Blake Lively vs. Justin Baldoni: Who Really Won the Settlement?

Hollywood loves a good drama — and the nearly two-year legal war between Blake Lively and Justin Baldoni delivered exactly that. But when the dust settled on May 4, 2026, just two weeks before trial was set to begin, both sides rushed to claim victory. So who actually won?

The answer, like most things in Hollywood, is complicated.

## What Happened?

Lively accused Baldoni — her *It Ends With Us* co-star and director — of sexual harassment on set, fostering a hostile work environment, and orchestrating a coordinated smear campaign against her. Baldoni denied everything and fired back with a $400 million defamation countersuit, which was thrown out by a judge in June 2025.

Then, just weeks before trial, a federal judge dismissed 10 of Lively's 13 claims — including every sexual harassment and defamation allegation. Only three narrower claims remained: retaliation, aiding and abetting retaliation, and breach of contract. With the biggest emotional claims gone, both sides had a reason to settle.

## Baldoni's Camp Says They Won

Baldoni's attorney called it "a win and total victory for the Wayfarer parties." His argument? Lively settled because she knew she was going to lose in court. On paper, that's a reasonable reading — she walked away with no financial compensation, and her most serious allegations never made it to a jury.

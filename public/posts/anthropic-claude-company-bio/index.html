Anthropic & Claude: The Company, the Founders, and the AI Built for Safety

Who built Claude AI? Learn everything about Anthropic, its founders Dario and Daniela Amodei, and the story behind the AI assistant built to be safe and helpful.
May 2, 2026 · 7 min

In a world racing to build the most powerful AI, one company decided to slow down and ask a more important question: how do we build AI that is actually safe? That company is Anthropic, and their AI assistant Claude has become one of the most trusted and capable AI tools in the world. Here is the complete story of Anthropic, its founders, and the AI they built.

What is Anthropic?#

Anthropic is an American AI safety company founded in 2021. Unlike most AI companies that focus primarily on capability, Anthropic was founded with a specific mission — to build AI systems that are safe, beneficial, and understandable.

The company conducts pioneering research into AI safety while simultaneously building and deploying Claude, one of the most capable AI assistants available today. Anthropic believes that the best way to ensure AI safety is to be at the frontier of AI development itself.

Company facts:

  • Founded: 2021
  • Headquarters: San Francisco, California, USA
  • CEO: Dario Amodei
  • Valuation: Over $18 billion (2024)
  • Employees: Over 500
  • Primary product: Claude AI assistant

The Founders of Anthropic#

Anthropic was founded by former OpenAI employees who left to start a company with a stronger focus on AI safety. The core founding team brought world-class expertise in AI research, policy, and business.

Dario Amodei — CEO & Co-Founder#

Dario Amodei is the CEO of Anthropic and one of the most respected figures in the AI safety community. Born and raised in the United States, he developed a passion for science and mathematics from an early age.

Dario completed his undergraduate degree in Physics at Princeton University and went on to earn a PhD in Computational Neuroscience from the University of California, San Diego. His background in neuroscience gave him a unique perspective on how intelligence works — both biological and artificial.

Before founding Anthropic, Dario served as VP of Research at OpenAI, where he oversaw the development of GPT-2 and GPT-3 — two of the most important language models ever created. During his time at OpenAI, he became increasingly concerned about the safety implications of increasingly powerful AI systems.

In 2021, Dario left OpenAI along with several colleagues to found Anthropic with a clear mission: to build AI that is safe, interpretable, and steerable. Under his leadership, Anthropic has pioneered Constitutional AI — a groundbreaking technique for training AI systems to be helpful, harmless, and honest.

Daniela Amodei — President & Co-Founder#

Daniela Amodei is the President of Anthropic and Dario’s sister. She is responsible for the business operations, go-to-market strategy, and overall growth of the company.

Before co-founding Anthropic, Daniela served as VP of Operations at OpenAI, where she built and scaled the operational infrastructure of the company. Her expertise in business operations has been instrumental in transforming Anthropic from a research lab into a commercially successful AI company.

Daniela is widely regarded as one of the most influential business leaders in the AI industry. She has spoken extensively about the importance of responsible AI development and the need for the private sector to take AI safety seriously.

Other Key Co-Founders#

Anthropic’s founding team also includes several other former OpenAI researchers who brought deep technical expertise to the company:

Tom Brown — One of the lead researchers behind GPT-3, the model that demonstrated the extraordinary capabilities of large language models.

Chris Olah — A world-renowned AI interpretability researcher who has done pioneering work on understanding how neural networks actually work internally.

Sam McCandlish — A physicist and AI researcher who focuses on understanding the theoretical foundations of deep learning.

Jack Clark — Former Policy Director at OpenAI who focuses on the policy and societal implications of advanced AI systems.

Jared Kaplan — A physicist whose research on scaling laws helped the field understand how AI capabilities grow with more data and computation.

The Story of Claude#

Claude is Anthropic’s flagship AI assistant, named after Claude Shannon — the mathematician and electrical engineer who founded information theory and is considered the father of the digital age.

Anthropic first released Claude to the public in March 2023, initially through a waitlist before making it widely available. Claude was built using a technique Anthropic developed called Constitutional AI.

What is Constitutional AI?

Constitutional AI is a revolutionary approach to training AI systems to be safe and beneficial. Instead of relying solely on human feedback to shape AI behavior, Anthropic created a set of principles — a “constitution” — that guides how Claude responds.

This constitution includes values like being helpful, being honest, avoiding harmful outputs, and respecting human autonomy. Claude is trained to evaluate its own responses against these principles and improve them accordingly.

Claude’s evolution:

  • Claude 1 (2023) — The first public version, notable for its thoughtful and nuanced responses
  • Claude 2 (2023) — Dramatically improved capabilities, including the ability to read and analyze extremely long documents
  • Claude 3 (2024) — A family of three models (Haiku, Sonnet, and Opus) offering different balances of speed and capability
  • Claude 3.5 (2024) — Claude 3.5 Sonnet set new benchmarks across coding, reasoning, and analysis tasks
  • Claude 4 (2025-2026) — The latest generation, featuring Claude Sonnet 4 and Claude Opus 4 with even more advanced reasoning and multimodal capabilities

What Makes Claude Different?#

Claude stands out from other AI assistants in several important ways:

Safety First — Anthropic’s entire company is built around AI safety. Claude is designed from the ground up to be helpful, harmless, and honest. It is more likely to acknowledge uncertainty, refuse genuinely harmful requests, and provide balanced perspectives than competing AI systems.

Long Context Window — Claude can read and analyze extremely long documents — entire books, legal contracts, or large codebases — without losing context. This makes it particularly valuable for research, legal work, and software development.

Writing Quality — Claude is widely praised for producing natural, nuanced, and engaging writing. Many professional writers and content creators prefer Claude for long-form writing tasks.

Honesty — Claude is designed to be transparent about its limitations and uncertainties. It is less likely to confidently state incorrect information — a problem known as hallucination — than some competing models.

Personality — Claude has a distinctive personality characterized by intellectual curiosity, warmth, and a genuine desire to be helpful. Many users find interactions with Claude to feel more natural and engaging than with other AI assistants.

Amazon’s Major Investment#

In 2023, Amazon announced a major investment in Anthropic of up to $4 billion — one of the largest investments in AI history. This partnership made Amazon Web Services (AWS) Anthropic’s primary cloud provider and gave Amazon’s Alexa and other products access to Claude’s capabilities.

Google has also invested approximately $300 million in Anthropic, reflecting the broader tech industry’s recognition of Claude as one of the leading AI systems in the world.

These investments have given Anthropic the resources to compete at the frontier of AI development while maintaining its commitment to safety research.

Anthropic’s Safety Research#

Beyond building Claude, Anthropic is one of the world’s leading AI safety research organizations. Some of their most important research areas include:

Interpretability — Understanding what is actually happening inside AI models when they process information. This research aims to make AI systems more transparent and understandable.

Alignment — Ensuring that AI systems pursue goals that are genuinely aligned with human values, even as they become more capable.

Scaling Laws — Understanding how AI capabilities change as models become larger and are trained on more data.

Red Teaming — Systematically trying to find ways that AI systems can be misused or can produce harmful outputs, so those vulnerabilities can be addressed.

The Future of Anthropic and Claude#

Anthropic occupies a unique position in the AI industry — a company that genuinely believes it may be building one of the most transformative and potentially dangerous technologies in human history, yet presses forward anyway. Their reasoning is that if powerful AI is coming regardless, it is better to have safety-focused organizations at the frontier.

Claude continues to evolve rapidly, with each new version bringing improvements in reasoning, coding, writing, and multimodal capabilities. In 2026, Claude is used by millions of individuals and thousands of businesses worldwide for everything from coding assistance and document analysis to creative writing and customer service.

Final Thoughts#

Anthropic and Claude represent a different vision of what AI development can look like — one where safety and capability go hand in hand rather than being in opposition. The Amodei siblings and their team have built not just a remarkable AI product, but an entire research agenda aimed at ensuring that the most powerful AI systems humanity builds are ones we can trust.

Whether you use Claude for work, study, or creative projects, you are benefiting from years of careful research and a genuine commitment to building AI that is good for everyone.
